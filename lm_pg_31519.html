Online resources

What free resources are available for improving my language skills?

Below you will find links to some useful resources for studying.
Please note that most require you to create an account, but it is only so that you can keep track of your own learning, save your progress and the website can tailor the tasks to your level and previous work. Thus registering means you have a more systematic approach to learning English. 

Vocabulary

CRAM is great for creating your own flashcards (or using someone else's) (no account needed)
QUIZLET also lets you create your own flashcards and creates other tasks around the same vocabulary lists (or you can use someone else's thematic lists)
FREE RICE is an addictive vocabulary game, where a grain of rice is donated to the UN World Food Programme for every word you know or guess correctly (no account needed)
KNOWORD is another addictive vocabulary game where you need to type a word based on the given definition and the first letter (no account needed)
PHRASEUM is a tool for learning phrases for different situations. You can choose a topic and see the most frequently used phrases from that sphare (no account needed) 
JUST-THE-WORD is a useful site for looking up word collocations and combinations - when you type in a wor, you see lists of the most usual combinations (with prepositions, collocations, etc.) (no account needed)


Reading and listening

COMMON LIT features a huge collection of graded texts (for different levels) on a variety of topics together with an accompanying audio and comprehension questions. You can also translate the text into main world languages (including Russian) (account needed for some features only)
READ LISTEN LEARN features a collection of graded readers (texts adjusted to different levels) with audio files, so you can read and listen at the same time
ESL BITS is a site with an impressive collection of classical novels, different short stories, speeches and articles that you can read and listen to at the same time. (no account needed)
LISTENNOTES is an excellent podcast search engine where you can look up episodes on topics that interest you (no account needed)
ESL VIDEO is a collection of videos with comprehension questions for different levels (no account needed)


Writing

WRITING SPARKS gives you prompts for writing different types of texts with specific tasks included (no account needed)
WRITE&IMPROVE provides many exam-type writing tasks (letters, essays, reports) that are automatically graded and corrected (no account needed unless you want to record your progress)


Spelling and pronunciation

SPELLING BEE is an excellent site where you can practice spelling words at different levels - you hear a word and have to type it
SPEECHLING is great for improving your pronunciation - you see a written phrase and need to record yourself saying it, then you can listen to a native speaker saying the same phrase and compare your versions
YOUGLISH is a cool search tool that looks up any word or phrase you type in from YouTube videos so you can hear how it is pronounced by the British or Americans or other nationalities. (no account needed)
FORVO is a pronunciation search engine where you can type in a word and look up how it is pronounced by real Americans and Brits (no account needed)


Communication

KIALO offers numerous different debates (new ones added daily) where you can participate by reading other people's views and posting your own, or you can even start your own debate
QUORA is a very popular collection of forums on different topics, where you can post and read questions and asnwers and engage in discussions 
The best idea would be to find an English-speaking forum on the topic that interests you and start reading and communicating there. This is the best way to improve your colloquial and informal language as well as get acquainted with relevant slang.


Other tools

SMMRY creates an automatic summary of any text that you need to read (no account needed)
SYNTAX HIGHLIGHTER tells you what part of speech (noun, verb, adjective, etc.) any word in your pasted text is (no account needed)
READLANG is an extension you can add to your browser (personal devices only!) that translates words on any web-page from one language to another (also into Estonian!) 
WORD HIPPO is an interesting tool for creative needs - you can type in a word and look up synonyms, antonyms, rhyming words, word forms, and also search for words starting or ending with, or containing specific letters or number of letters (no account needed)
REWORDIFY is a useful tool that simplifies any text you paste on the site. It also provides a list of the more difficult words with definitions. (no account needed)
